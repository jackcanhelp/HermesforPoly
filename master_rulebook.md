# Hermes Master Rulebook

## General
* Balance short-term catalysts with market volatility, considering institutional buying pressure, spot market momentum, and external factors to avoid diverging predictions.
* Continuously monitor and update analysis with new information, including social sentiment and potential upset factors, to avoid overconfidence and ensure a balanced approach.
* Leverage crowd sentiment to adjust probability assessments, but avoid over-indexing on extreme positivity or negativity due to confirmation bias or hometown fervor.
* Be cautious of anchoring bias, action bias, recency bias, and confirmation bias when making predictions.
* Prioritize concrete, match-specific data over broader trends and sentiment when making predictions, but also consider the value of social sentiment and broader trends in certain situations.
* Avoid under-weighting the impact of new information or developments that become available to the market after initial assessment.

## Geopolitics
* Balance recent events with underlying complexities, avoiding overemphasizing short-term developments in high-stakes situations.
* Recognize the potential for external factors to impact probability assessments and the influence of external brokers on conflict resolution.
* Consider the historical context and long-term distrust in geopolitical relations when evaluating the impact of short-term diplomatic efforts.

## Crypto
* Institutional buying pressure and spot market momentum drive cryptocurrency market shifts, reinforcing bullish sentiment.
* Consider the broader market context, including regulatory changes and market corrections.
* Recognize the importance of considering the potential for market volatility to reassess the sustainability of rallies.

## Psychology
* Recognize the impact of extreme market sentiment on probability assessments and market movements.
* Factor in the influence of crowd sentiment to reinforce probability assessments, but avoid overconfidence due to overemphasizing recent advancements.
* Be aware of the potential for market sentiment to shift rapidly in response to new information or developments.
* Consider the potential for social sentiment and broader trends to be a more reliable predictor of outcome than waiting for specific details.
* Avoid over-reliance on specific, detailed information and consider the value of general trends and sentiment in predicting success.

## Sports
* Consider multiple factors, including recent form, past performances, and team dynamics, when making predictions.
* Prioritize match-specific data, including recent team performance, injuries, and opponent strengths, when evaluating team performance.
* Continuously update and refine assessments based on evolving information and crowd sentiment.
* Avoid over-weighting a team's past successes and under-weighting the strength of their opponent.
* Be cautious of anchoring bias when lacking information, and consider the possibility that a lack of information or neutral sentiment does not necessarily imply a low probability of success.
* Monitor and update analysis based on new information, such as team injuries, suspensions, and social sentiment, to avoid confirmation bias and refine predictions.
