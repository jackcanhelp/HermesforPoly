# Hermes Master Rulebook

## General
* Consider the broader context in high-stakes, complex situations, avoiding overemphasis on short-term developments.
* Strike a balance between recent events and nuanced understanding of underlying complexities and risks.
* Crowd sentiment can be a valuable indicator, especially in situations with high uncertainty.
* Institutional buying pressure and spot market momentum can significantly impact market shifts and probability estimates.
* Balance short-term market sentiment with long-term geopolitical and economic trends to form accurate probability assessments.
* External factors, such as market volatility and potential for renewed hostilities, can impact probability assessments.

## Geopolitics
* Weigh both security threats and potential positive developments, such as unreported diplomatic progress or international pressure, in assessing conflict resolution probabilities.
* Market shifts can indicate new information or developments that may not be publicly available, requiring adaptability in probability assessments.
* Diplomatic efforts and economic pressure can outweigh security concerns and drive conflict resolution.
* Consider the potential for external factors, such as market volatility and potential for renewed hostilities, to impact conflict resolution probabilities.

## Crypto
* Institutional buying pressure and spot market momentum can significantly impact cryptocurrency market shifts and probability estimates.
* Balance short-term market sentiment with long-term trends to form accurate probability assessments.

## Psychology
* Recognize the impact of extreme market sentiment (e.g., FUD/PANIC) on probability assessments and market movements.
* Maintain a nuanced understanding of market dynamics, considering both visible and potential underlying factors influencing market shifts.
* Crowd sentiment can be a valuable indicator, especially in situations with high uncertainty.
