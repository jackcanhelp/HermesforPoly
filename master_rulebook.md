# Hermes Master Rulebook

## General
* Balance short-term catalysts with market volatility, considering institutional buying pressure, spot market momentum, and external factors to avoid diverging predictions.
* Integrate unforeseen developments into probability assessments, recognizing potential over-optimism and under-weighting of risks.
* Leverage crowd sentiment to adjust probability assessments, especially in situations with high uncertainty.
* Consider the market's tendency to consolidate gains before making further moves.
* Avoid over-indexing on expert consensus, news articles, and recent performances, and stay up-to-date with official statements, economic indicators, and team news.
* Factor in the potential for surprise decisions, injuries, tactical surprises, and unforeseen developments that can impact probability assessments.
* Recognize the importance of considering multiple perspectives, staying aware of potential blind spots, and continually updating analysis to reflect changing market conditions.
* Consider the strategic implications of a company's actions, including exclusive access and strategic partnerships, when evaluating competitive advantage.
* Be cautious of anchoring on current prices and underestimating the potential for rapid price movements driven by geopolitical events.

## Geopolitics
* Balance recent events with underlying complexities, avoiding overemphasizing short-term developments in high-stakes situations.
* Recognize the potential for external factors to impact probability assessments and the influence of external brokers on conflict resolution.
* Leverage crowd sentiment as a valuable indicator to reinforce probability assessments, particularly in situations with high stakes and complex geopolitics.
* Consider diplomatic efforts, economic pressure, and external brokers when assessing probability.
* Factor in the potential for regime changes, geopolitical tensions, and unforeseen developments to impact market sentiment and probability assessments.
* Accurately calibrate the influence of geopolitical events on market predictions, avoiding underestimation of their potential impact.

## Crypto
* Institutional buying pressure and spot market momentum drive cryptocurrency market shifts, reinforcing bullish sentiment.
* Consider the broader market context, including regulatory changes and market corrections.
* Recognize the importance of considering the potential for market volatility to reassess the sustainability of rallies.
* Continuously monitor market sentiment and adapt to new information to refine probability assessments.
* Avoid under-weighting the impact of institutional buying pressure and spot market momentum on market sentiment.

## Psychology
* Recognize the impact of extreme market sentiment on probability assessments and market movements.
* Factor in the influence of crowd sentiment to reinforce probability assessments, particularly in situations with high stakes and complex geopolitics.
* Be aware of the potential for market sentiment to shift rapidly in response to new information or developments.
* Recognize the importance of maintaining a nuanced understanding of underlying complexities and risks, avoiding over-indexing on short-term developments.
* Avoid overconfidence in a company's market position due to overemphasizing recent advancements and underestimating the pace of competition.

## AI and Technology
* Recognize the rapid pace of development and innovation in the AI sector, and the potential for other companies to make significant advancements in a short period.
* Avoid over-weighting the current capabilities of a single company or model, and under-weighting the potential for other competitors to make breakthroughs.
* Consider the uncertainty and volatility of the AI landscape when making probability assessments.
* Recognize the importance of accessibility, open-sourcing, and community-driven development in the AI sector, but also consider the strategic implications of exclusive access and controlled release strategies.
* Factor in the potential for the market to prioritize accessibility and open-source releases over exclusive, restricted models, while also valuing the competitive advantage provided by strategic partnerships and controlled release strategies.

## Sports
* Consider multiple factors, including recent form, past performances, and team dynamics, when making predictions.
* Avoid over-indexing on outdated information and be cautious of recency bias when evaluating team performance.
* Stay up-to-date with the latest information and seek out relevant data to inform predictions, rather than relying on general trends or outdated statistics.
